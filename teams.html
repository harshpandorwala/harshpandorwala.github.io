The Problem

Work is the reason your team stops showing up for themselves.

Every high performer knows what good habits look like. They have read the books, downloaded the apps, started the routines. But then a deadline hits, a meeting runs long, a week goes by, and the habits quietly disappear.

The problem is not discipline. The problem is environment. When the people around you are not doing it, it is almost impossible to sustain it alone.

This challenge changes that. For seven days, your entire team commits together. The habits are simple. The time investment is small. The impact, when done as a group, is something completely different.

The Seven Habits

Simple on paper.
Powerful together.

Each habit takes 7 minutes. Together they take 49 minutes a day. Tap each habit to learn more.

01
Movement
7 minutes
A simple but powerful reset. Step outside for a 7-minute walk or find a quiet corner to stretch. No guidance needed, just move your body and let your mind breathe.
+
02
Meditation
7 minutes
A simple guided practice. No experience needed. Builds the kind of presence and clarity that makes everything else at work easier.
+
03
Journaling
7 minutes
One reflection prompt a day. Builds the self-awareness that separates reactive professionals from intentional ones. Not fluff, real clarity.
+
04
Mindful Break
7 minutes
No phone, no laptop, no agenda. Take a nap, sit in silence, or simply do nothing. Seven minutes to fully step away and let yourself reset however feels right.
+
05
Hydration
All day
Seven glasses of water tracked consciously. Most people are mildly dehydrated during work hours without realising how much it affects concentration and mood.
+
06
Learning
7 minutes
Something new, every day. A concept, a perspective, a page. Keeps the mind curious and growing beyond the immediate demands of work.
+
07
Reading
7 minutes
Seven minutes of reading every day. Not screens, not scrolling. A book, a page, an idea. Small and consistent, it builds a mind that keeps growing.
+

Why I Built This

Time to change
the pattern.

After working with individuals who wanted to change their life and build better habits, I noticed something that was a common hurdle between all of them. It was never a lack of desire. It was the environment they were returning to every single day, their work routine, their team culture, whether in an office or working remotely, that quietly pulled them back.

When a whole team does this together, something different happens. The environment itself shifts. Habits that felt hard alone start to feel natural. That is the power of the group, and it is why I built this challenge.
